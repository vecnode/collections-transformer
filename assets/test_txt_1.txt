William Shakespeare was an English playwright and poet.
He was born in Stratford-upon-Avon in 1564.
He is often called the Bard of Avon.
Shakespeare wrote tragedies, comedies, and histories.
His plays were performed at the Globe Theatre.
Hamlet is one of his most studied tragedies.
Macbeth explores ambition, guilt, and fate.
Othello examines jealousy and manipulation.
King Lear reflects on power, family, and madness.
Romeo and Juliet tells a famous story of young love.
A Midsummer Night's Dream is a celebrated comedy.
Twelfth Night features disguise, wit, and mistaken identity.
His sonnets remain important in English literature.
Shakespeare's language shaped many modern expressions.
His works have been translated into many languages.
The authorship of his works has been debated by some.
Most scholars still credit him as the primary author.
His characters are known for emotional depth.
His themes continue to feel relevant today.
Shakespeare's legacy remains central to world theatre.
